Dear Data

A project between Giorgia Lupi and Stefanie Posavec. They would send postcards to eachother and each would have a different graph or visualisation of some kind of data from their lives. Always presented with a key so as for the other to understand. This project went on for a year, and it shows many different, creative ways to present data. I love the hand drawn roughness to it, yet still so meticulous

Nathalie Miebech

Nathalie Miebech is an artist that creates beautiful, colourful sculptures that represent different sets of data. Some are based on extreme weather events such as Hurricane Maria and Hurricane Sandy. I'm particularly drawn to the 3D Musical Scores.

Hans Haacke

Haacke is a conceptual artist from Germany who involves data in his work. They feel so tangible and grounded. The data has become a kind of material that adds a further bias to how we look at it.Dealing with political and social themes, he shows how data and art provide an effective combination for activism.

Chirming

Visualisation of bird sounds by Sukgo. I love how delicate and intricate the patterns are, they challenge assumptions about data visualisation. The shapes are organic.

W.E.B Du Bois

Du Bois used data visualisation as activism more than a hundred years ago. He explored many new chart types, and they still feel contemporary. A lot of them are interested in the racial story of America.
